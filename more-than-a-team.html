The gap

Most recruitment posts tell families what the club wants.

Parents are often reading those posts from a very different place. They are not only asking whether there is a space. They are asking whether the environment is right for their child.

What clubs usually say

  • We need players.
  • Must have experience.
  • Trials start soon.
  • Message the coach.
  • Spaces available in selected age groups.

What parents need to know

  • Will my child feel safe and understood here?
  • How does the club respond when children struggle?
  • Will anxiety be mistaken for attitude?
  • Will neurodivergent players be supported calmly?
  • Will this environment build confidence or slowly damage it?
Parents don’t just choose a team. They choose an environment.

Copy and paste

The difference is in the wording.

Choose a template, copy it, then add the club name, age group, training night and contact details.

Younger age groups

At this age, sport should build confidence, not pressure.

We’re welcoming new players and families for the upcoming season. Our focus is enjoyment, development, friendship and creating an environment where children feel safe to try, make mistakes and grow.

Our club has access to FMHA resources that help us better understand young players’ wellbeing, confidence, anxiety and neurodiversity.

If you’re looking for a team where your child can enjoy the game and feel supported, we’d love to hear from you.
How to use this

Keep the tone warm, then add the basics: club name, age group, training location, session time and contact details. Avoid overpromising. Show the intent and point people towards a conversation.

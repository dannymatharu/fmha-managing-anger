Slide seven
Officials matter

A referee can calm a game — or accidentally inflame it.

The training gives officials a better read on neurodiverse behaviours, distress, overstimulation and the value of simple pre-match factfinding.

The aim isn’t to ignore the laws of the game. It’s to understand the player in front of you while keeping football fair, safe and playable.

FMHA Vault
Slide eight
Duty of care

Inclusion needs a record, not just good intentions.

Clubs are expected to make reasonable adjustments when neurodivergent participants could be placed at a substantial disadvantage. Training helps people understand what that means in normal football situations.

“
Good intentions are not a system.

Awareness, consistency and documentation protect players — and protect clubs.
